# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the Bucketwise Planner project and its community will be treated with respect and dignity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Be respectful.** Value each other's ideas, styles, and viewpoints.
- **Be direct but professional.** We are likely to have some discussions about what is best for Bucketwise Planner and user experience.
- **Be inclusive.** Seek diverse perspectives and work to include underrepresented groups.
- **Appreciate and accommodate our similarities and differences.** We come from many cultures and backgrounds. Cultural differences can enrich us.
- **Lead by example.** Match the behavior you want to see and acknowledge good examples when you see them.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment.** Intimidation, stalking, doxxing, or sexual harassment of any kind.
- **Discrimination.** Slurs, insults, or prejudiced comments based on protected characteristics.
- **Threats.** Threatening violence or harm.
- **Spamming.** Flooding channels with off-topic or repetitive content.
- **Trolling.** Deliberately disruptive comments meant to provoke anger.

## Enforcement

If someone engages in behavior that violates this code of conduct, the community organizers may take action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the community.

- Responding within 48 hours
- Investigating fairly and impartially

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces associated with Bucketwise Planner, including:
- GitHub issues and pull requests
- GitHub Discussions
- Project documentation
- Community chat or forums
